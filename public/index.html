Welcome! Thank you again for participating in our study. On this page, we present instructions on how to complete the trials. Please carefully read the following instructions as your performance is dependent on understanding the task.

Instructions

Our study involves three parts: “visual”, “tactile”, and “tabular”.

  • The “visual” part of the study will involve you inspecting some simple geometric shapes on a computer display. This condition has 56 trials. You will first identify the smaller visual encoding. Then, in a number between 0 and 100, you will estimate what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.
  • The “tactile” part will involve inspecting some geometric shapes on embossed paper by touching the paper with your hands and answering questions. You will be blindfolded with a piece of cloth for this part of the study. This condition has 56 trials. You will first locate the tactile pair on the embossed paper. Then, same to the "visual" condition, you will answer orally to the study administrator which one is the smaller encoding. Finally, you will answer to the study administrator a number between 0 and 100 of what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.
  • Finally, the “tabular” part will involve viewing a data table on a computer display or using a screen reader and answering questions.  This condition has 8 trials. In this condition, you will only need to answer to the study administrator a number between 0 and 100 of what the proportion of the smaller encoding is to the larger one.

In a single trial across all three parts, you will compare a pair of values encoded in visual, tactile, tabular form. Upon comparing the patterns, you will answer to the researcher:

  • which one in the pair is of the smaller quantity.
  • Then, you will answer again and provide a number between 0 and 100 to indicate the proportion of the smaller element (from your previous answer) to the larger one.
Note that since both “visual” and “tabular” parts will be conducted using a computer, you will complete these two parts together on a computer in random order. You will perform the “tactile” part of our study separately on paper while blind-folded.

Encoding Types

In our study, we encoded numerical value pairs using various visual/tactile elements. The below sections will explain how values are encoded and represented in these encodings. Note that "visual" and "tactile" present same types of encodings on different mediums. It is important for you to understand the relationship between numerical value and encoding types.

Lines

In encoding type “lines”, numerical values are represented in lengths. You will inspect the pair of lines directly by comparing their difference in lengths. An example of a pair of two lines encodings:

Areas

In encoding type "areas", numerical values are represented in areas in circles. The area of a circle is the measure of the amount of space enclosed by the circle. You will inspect the pair of circles and compare their difference in areas. An example of a pair of two area encodings:

In this example, "Area B" is smaller in its enclosed region than "Area A", thus it is the smaller element in the pair. "Area B" is 50% of "Area A" in area.

Angles

In encoding type "angles", numerical values are represented in the degree of angles. An angle is the geometric figure formed in between two straight lines. You will inspect the pair of angles and compare their difference in the degree of angles. An example of two angle encodings:

Curves

In encoding type "curves", numerical values are represented in the value of curvature. Curvature is a measure of how much a curve deviates from being a stright line. You will inspect the pair of curves and compare their difference in the value of curvature. An example of a pair of two curve encodings:

Position

In encoding type "positions", numerical values are represented by the location of points along a bounded axis or interval. In our example, we present a horizontal axis scaled into value from 0 to 100. The closer the distance from the point to the left boundary of the axis the smaller the value; the larger the distance the larger the value. You will inspect a pair of points on a common bounded horizontal axis. An example of a pair of position encodings:

In this example, "Position A" is smaller than "Position B" since it is closer in distance to the left boundary of the axis. "Position A" is 50% of "Position B" in the value of position.

Piecharts

In encoding type "Piecharts", numerical values are represented by the size of segment in the chart. The pie chart is a circular chart that is divided into sections, with each section representing a specific proportion. The size of each section is proportional to the value it represents. In our study, you will inspect two segments in a pie chart and compare their difference in size. An example of pie chart encodings:

In this example, "segment A" in the pie is smaller than "segment B" in the pie in the size. "Segment A" is 50% of "segment B" in size.

Barplots

In encoding type "Barplots", numerical values are represented by height of bars. In a bar plot, the values are represented using rectangular bars. The height of each bar corresponds to the value it represents. In our study, we present our pair of values between 0 and 100 in two bars. You will inspect the difference in height of the two bars. Tabular

In encoding type "Tabular", numerical values are directly presented in tables. You will directly compare and estimate what percentage the smaller value is of the larger in the pair. An example of encoding tabular:
